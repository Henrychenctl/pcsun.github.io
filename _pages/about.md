I am a surface and interface mechanics researcher driven by the challenge of controlling extreme wetting phenomena, capillarity, and directional liquid transport. Currently serving as a Postdoctoral Fellow in the [Department of Mechanical Engineering](https://www.polyu.edu.hk/me/) at [The Hong Kong Polytechnic University](https://www.polyu.edu.hk/) under the mentorship of [Prof. Zuankai Wang](https://www.polyu.edu.hk/me/people/academic-teaching-staff/wang-zuankai-prof/), my work centers on droplet-substrate interactions and fundamental microfluidics.

My academic journey began at Shandong University, leading to an Outstanding Master's Thesis award at the Nanjing University of Aeronautics and Astronautics under the supervision of [Prof. Xiuqing Hao](https://faculty.nuaa.edu.cn/haoxiuqing/en/jsxx/156939/jsxx/jsxx.htm). I then completed my Ph.D. studies in Mechanical Engineering at the City University of Hong Kong, co-supervised by [Prof. Steven Wang](https://www.cityu.edu.hk/stfprofile/stevenwang.htm) and [Prof. Zuankai Wang](https://www.polyu.edu.hk/me/people/academic-teaching-staff/wang-zuankai-prof/). During my doctoral research, I focused on achieving extreme liquid pressure resistance on armored reentrant super-omniphobic surfaces, enabling the spontaneous transport of highly viscous liquids, and exploring acousto-dewetting. Moving into my postdoctoral phase, I have expanded my toolkit to include gradient wettability patterning and under-liquid laser machining to better understand capillary self-pumping and droplet rebound limits.

Beyond the bench, my research has resulted in first- and co-first-author publications in journals including Nature Physics, Newton, Small Methods, Small, and Advanced Energy Materials. and mentoring junior graduate researchers. I welcome outreach regarding academic collaborations, peer review requests, or speaking opportunities.
